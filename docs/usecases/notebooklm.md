## The Grounded Research Partner

**Developer**: Google Labs

**Description**: NotebookLM uses a technology called RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) to "ground" the AI in your
specific documents. You upload PDFs, text files, or website links, and the AI becomes an expert on *only* that material.
It is famous for its "Audio Overview" feature, which turns your boring documents into an engaging, viral-style podcast.

**Key Features**:

- **Source Grounding**: The AI answers questions using *only* your uploaded sources. If the answer isn't in your files,
  it says "I don't know."
- **Inline Citations**: Every sentence it writes includes a citation number. Click it to jump to the exact paragraph in
  your PDF.
- **Audio Overviews**: Generates a stunningly realistic "Deep Dive" podcast where two AI hosts banter and discuss your
  documents.
- **Video Overviews**: Generates a realistic "Deep Dive" video of the selected documents.
- **Study Guides**: Automatically turns your notes into FAQs, timelines, or briefing documents.
- **Privacy**: Your data is used to answer your questions but is not used to train the public Google models.

**Best For**: Students, researchers, lawyers, and anyone who needs to understand complex documents quickly.

## What You Can Do (Use Cases)

### 1. The "Instant Podcast" (Viral Feature)

**Scenario**: You have a dry, 20-page report on "Quarterly Fiscal Regulations" and you don't want to read it.

* **Action**: Upload the PDF to NotebookLM and click **"Generate Audio Overview."**
* **Result**: In 5 minutes, you get a 10-minute MP3 of two enthusiastic hosts discussing the report, making jokes, and
  using analogies to explain the hard parts. It sounds exactly like a real radio show.

### 2. The "Exam Prepper"

**Scenario**: You have 300 slides from your university professor.

* **Action**: Upload the slide deck (PDF).
* **Prompt**: "Create a glossary of all the key terms defined in these slides and create a 10-question multiple choice
  quiz."
* **Result**: It generates a study guide based *strictly* on what the professor wrote, ensuring you study the right
  material.

### 3. The "Meeting Synthesizer"

**Scenario**: You have notes from 5 different strategy meetings.

* **Action**: Copy-paste the notes into a source.
* **Prompt**: "What are the conflicting opinions between the Marketing team and the Engineering team?"
* **Result**: It cross-references the files to find specific disagreements within your internal notes.
